Feb 2018:
First semester of the second year is at an end, and I'm proud of how I was able to finish it. I showcased a series I'm sure I'll continue to work on and show in the future. The project is called "Guilt" and is a self reflection about my boundries and pushing myself to showcase them. I have a triptych of religious imagery on cardboard cutout together with a mosaic grid of a tabboo lifestyle people are expected to keep for themselves and a video portrait of myself where I film myself for an hour and accompany with self reflective poetry. With this series I hope to reject the norm of displaying photography, reject the shame that hangs around the taboo's that are part of my life. And reject societal norms bestowed on us by religious history. I'm taking the feedback and observations I got from this seriously and it will help me improve my series.

Set up

STOFWOLK_Plek Oktober In october I stepped out of my comfort zone and participated in performance art. Together with Hanne Louisia and Mathilde Timmerman I looked into guilty pleasures, however small or large they might be. For us guilty pleasures came from societal presures and shame so we crafted a small otherworldly room people could come and visit and spectate as if we were a reality television show. Unable to interact with the outside for 4 hours in total people can tune in and out whenever they want. Just like reality tv, this will have its high's and lows in terms of entertainment.
